Why an engineers keeps a blog

The architecture diagrams stay on the internal wiki; the trade-offs get argued out in a meeting and then quietly forgotten. This is where I do the opposite — work through ideas in the open, where I can be wrong in public and learn faster for it.

What I'll write about

Roughly the things I've learned new'

  • Decisions and the reasoning behind them, not just the outcome.
  • The boring constraints — compliance, cost, change management — that shape real architecture.
  • Occasionally, a war story with the names filed off.
The best architecture writing explains a choice well enough that a reader can disagree with it.
